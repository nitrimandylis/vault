## Immediate Next Steps (Priority Order)

1. **UCL IMB personal statement** — Start drafting now. Focus on tech-business interest, specific projects, tools, reading, experiences. Math HL 5 means the rest of application carries extra weight.
2. **BOT preparation (Bocconi)** — Register for a BOT sitting. Target 35+ minimum; 40+ is strong for BEMACS. Bocconi Round 1 deadline: September.
3. **Tech-business experience** — Build concrete experience before applications: internship at tech/consulting firm, personal data project, business competition.
4. **Wider reading** — Read around information management and digital strategy (The Lean Startup, Competing in the Age of AI, HBR articles on digital transformation).
5. **IB score optimisation** — Secure a 6 in Maths in May exams. Raise high 6s in Business, CS, English, Global Politics to 7s.
6. **UCAS application timing** — Submit as early as possible (October/November). UCL IMB is oversubscribed. Early submission is an advantage.
7. **EU applications (parallel)** — Bocconi (Round 1, September), KU Leuven, UvA all have separate processes. Run in parallel — do not wait for UK outcomes. UvA deadline: 1 May (rolling).
